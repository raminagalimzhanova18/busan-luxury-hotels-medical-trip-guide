01 — Seomyeon, Busanjin-gu
Lotte Hotel Busan — City Centre
~$165–$300 / night
✓ Under Budget ★ Top Pick 🏥 Clinic Hub
💊 Proximity to Clinics
  • Seomyeon Medical Street5-min walk · 200+ certified clinics
  • Renewme Skin Clinic~5-min walk · near Seomyeon Station Exit 9
  • JRYN Dermatology~8-min walk · Seomyeon Station Exit 16
  • DayBeau Clinic Seomyeon~8-min walk · Seomyeon district
  • Dong-Eui Medical Center~10-min taxi · Busanjin-gu
📍 Location
Seomyeon — Busan's central downtown, intersection of Subway Lines 1 & 2. 5-min walk to Seomyeon Station. Directly connected underground to Lotte Department Store. 30-min drive to KTX Station, 30-min to Gimhae Airport. Dense urban grid with restaurants, cafes, and shopping at every turn.
🛏 Room Quality
650 rooms; largest luxury hotel in Busan. Spacious air-conditioned rooms with down comforters, pillow menu, deep-soak bathtubs, robes, bidets. Club Floor rooms offer city panorama views. Individually furnished. Some rooms show age but premium tiers are well-maintained and recently renovated.
☕ Breakfast
Buffet breakfast at La Seine (surcharge unless booked with breakfast plan). Club Floor guests get breakfast included in the Club Lounge (6:30–10:00 AM), plus Afternoon Tea and Cocktail service. Very generous spread with Korean, Japanese, and Western options.
✦ Amenities
Indoor & outdoor pools, casino, duty-free store, golf driving range, Josiane Laure Spa, fitness centre (surcharge from Jan 2025), 4 restaurants (Korean, Japanese, Chinese, buffet), 2 bars, 12 banquet halls, on-site department store. Concierge, valet.
⊕ Overall Verdict
Ideal for a medical trip. Seomyeon is Busan's unrivalled medical and beauty clinic hub — you can walk to 200+ certified clinics from the hotel front door. The hotel sits at the best-connected subway intersection in the city, making it trivially easy to reach any clinic in Busan. Price comfortably under budget. Service is polished. The scale (650 rooms) means availability is excellent.
02 — Haeundae-gu
Paradise Hotel Busan — Haeundae Beach
~$140–$250 / night
✓ Under Budget
💊 Proximity to Clinics
  • Inje Univ. Haeundae Paik Hospital~10-min taxi · 875 Haeun-Daero
  • Haeundae-area skin clinicsSeveral within 15-min walk
  • Seomyeon Medical District~20-min subway (Line 2) to Seomyeon

Haeundae has good clinic coverage but far fewer options than Seomyeon. Subway access to Seomyeon is straightforward (Line 2, ~20 min).
📍 Location
Directly on Haeundae Beach. 10-min walk to Haeundae Subway Station (Line 2). Close to Busan Aquarium, Paradise Casino. Upscale beach area; more resort feel than urban medical hub. 30-min drive to Gimhae Airport.
🛏 Room Quality
Sea-view balcony rooms available; spacious and well-appointed with aroma bath salts, flat-screen TVs, full private bathrooms. Some rooms show signs of age in bathrooms per guest reviews. Comfortable beds, good overall standard. More resort-leisure than business-precision.
☕ Breakfast
Buffet breakfast available at surcharge (KRW 63,000 / ~$47 per adult). Not included in base rate. Multiple dining venues on-site including Nyx Grill & Wine, Asian/Chinese, bakery.
✦ Amenities
Casino (international passport holders only), outdoor hot spring, Ocean Spa Cimer, large outdoor pool, indoor golf range, fitness center, dart boards, playground. Multiple restaurants and bar. Free parking.
⊕ Overall Verdict
Good but second-tier for a medical trip. Beach location is beautiful but clinics are a 20-min subway or taxi ride away. Reasonable price, solid amenities and beach access make it excellent for a leisure trip. For medical focus, the extra commute adds friction to early-morning or post-procedure returns.

Dongnae-gu is a quieter residential district — fewer specialist private clinics within walking distance compared to Seomyeon or Haeundae.
📍 Location
23 Geumganggongwon-ro, Dongnae-gu. 8.4 miles from city centre. Suburb feel; near Geumgang Botanical Garden. 10-min walk to Myeongnyun subway station (Line 1). 10-min drive to Lotte Department Store. Near Beomeosa Temple. More residential and peaceful — not a medical hub.
🛏 Room Quality
240 rooms with Korean traditional furniture touches and "90s luxury" aesthetic. Comfortable beds, spacious bathrooms, mini-bar, bidet, bath tub. Garden-view rooms available. Some feel the decor is dated; the spa/hot springs are the true draw. Well-maintained overall.
☕ Breakfast
Buffet breakfast included (6:30–10:00 AM). Guests consistently rate it as "delicious." Korean, Japanese, and Western options. Also has Italian restaurant (Ristorante), Korean restaurant (Nae Dang), and 3 bars/lounges. Coffee shop on-site.
✦ Amenities
Heosimcheong Hot Springs (Asia's largest thermal spa, 50% discount for guests ~KRW 7,500), sauna, health club, fitness center, 3 restaurants, 3 bars, free parking, hair salon, car rental, business center. Post-procedure recuperation in hot springs is genuinely excellent.
⊕ Overall Verdict
Niche appeal: if your medical purpose is spa, wellness or thermal therapy, this is the best pick by far. Heosimcheong Hot Springs is genuinely remarkable for post-procedure recovery. However, it's the most removed from private medical clinics of all seven hotels. Best for recovery stays rather than pre/post appointment logistics.

ℹ️ Brand Clarification
ℹ️ What Is "Marriott Busan"?

There is no standalone hotel called "Marriott Busan" in the city. Marriott International operates the following properties in Busan under its Marriott Bonvoy umbrella:


  • The Westin Josun Busan5-star · Haeundae · $174–$350/night — reviewed above (#05)
  • Fairfield by Marriott Busan3-star · Haeundae · ~$84–$130/night — budget tier, not luxury
  • Fairfield by Marriott Busan Songdo Beach3-star · Seo-gu · ~$87–$120/night — budget/mid tier

The flagship luxury Marriott Bonvoy property in Busan is The Westin Josun Busan (reviewed as Hotel #05). If you were searching for a full-service Marriott Hotels & Resorts brand property (e.g. JW Marriott, Autograph Collection), none currently exist in Busan as of 2025 — Seoul has JW Marriott properties but Busan does not.


Recommendation: If your intent was The Westin Josun Busan as the "Marriott property," see Hotel #05 above. For a pure Marriott brand, consider The Westin your closest equivalent.
